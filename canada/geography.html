Geography & Nature

A land of vast landscapes, from coast to coast to coast.

The Canadian Rockies

Spanning British Columbia and Alberta, the Rockies offer some of the most dramatic mountain scenery on Earth, perfect for hiking and skiing.

The Great Lakes

Home to Niagara Falls and vast freshwater ecosystems, this region is a vital part of Canada's geography and industry.

The Arctic North

A land of tundra, ice, and polar bears. The Canadian North is a remote and beautiful frontier comprising Yukon, NWT, and Nunavut.

The Prairies

The breadbasket of Canada. Manitoba, Saskatchewan, and Alberta feature vast fields of wheat, canola, and big blue skies.

The Maritimes

Rugged coastlines, lighthouses, and fishing villages define the Atlantic provinces of Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, and PEI.

Boreal Forest

One of the world's largest intact forests, stretching across the country and providing a home for diverse wildlife.
